# Essay Writing Skill

## Core Structure

Every essay must follow a three-part structure:

### 1. Introduction (10-15% of total word count)
- **Hook**: Open with a compelling statement, question, or anecdote
- **Context**: Provide necessary background information
- **Thesis Statement**: A clear, arguable claim in 1-2 sentences
- The thesis must appear in the final sentence of the introduction

### 2. Body (70-80% of total word count)
- Each paragraph must have exactly ONE main idea
- **Topic sentence**: First sentence states the paragraph's claim
- **Evidence**: Support with data, quotes, examples, or logical reasoning
- **Analysis**: Explain HOW the evidence supports the claim
- **Transition**: Final sentence bridges to the next paragraph
- Use 3-5 body paragraphs for standard essays

### 3. Conclusion (10-15% of total word count)
- Restate the thesis in new words (do NOT copy the introduction)
- Summarize key arguments without introducing new evidence
- End with a thought-provoking statement, call to action, or broader implication

## Tone Rules

- Use formal register (no contractions, slang, or first person unless instructed)
- Prefer active voice over passive voice
- Vary sentence length: mix short punchy sentences with longer complex ones
- Avoid hedging language ("sort of", "kind of", "maybe")

## Quality Markers

- Every claim must be backed by at least one piece of evidence
- Counter-arguments should be acknowledged and refuted
- Transitions between paragraphs should feel natural, not formulaic
- The conclusion must feel like a destination, not an afterthought

## Common Pitfalls

- ❌ Starting with "In this essay, I will..."
- ❌ Using Wikipedia as a primary source
- ❌ Making sweeping generalizations without evidence
- ❌ Introducing new arguments in the conclusion
- ❌ Using "firstly, secondly, thirdly" as paragraph openers
